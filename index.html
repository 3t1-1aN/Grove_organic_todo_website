Designed to feel,
not just function

Dark forest-green glass over slow-drifting blobs. Cards lift on hover with spring easing; checkboxes and trays use a soft neumorphic raised/inset language — tactile without skeuomorphic chrome.

Memo breaks the pattern on purpose: warm paper on the same stage, so capture feels lighter than execution. Focus stays minimal — queue, timer, one clear next action.

Built into the app

Small rules,
big clarity

Grove nudges you toward a sustainable daily load — then gets out of the way with layouts and motion that match how you actually work.

Today cap
Five active slots max. The dot meter shows how full today is — amber when you hit the limit so backlog stays honest.
Four layouts
List for flow, Kanban by priority, Compact for density, Matrix for Eisenhower sorting — same tasks, different lens.
Focus modes
Daily Focus lines up today’s queue and session flow. Pomodoro adds timed work and break cycles when you need structure.
Memo wall
Handwritten paper cards for thoughts that are not quite tasks — pin, tilt, and clear without opening a second app.
Priority orbs
Tap the glowing dot for high, medium, or low. Color carries urgency without turning every row into a form.
Completion motion
Strike-through, slide-away dismiss, note crumple — finishes feel like release, with reduced-motion respect when you need it.
